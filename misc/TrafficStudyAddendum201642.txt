Memorandum
Date:           April 29, 2016
To:             Mr. Victor Gonzalez, Greystar
From:           Michelle Hunt, Vice President and Principal Associate
Subject:        Traffic Study Addendum for 1409 El Camino Real Residential Development,
                Redwood City, California - Intersection Turning Movement Data Comparison

This memorandum serves as an addendum to the traffic study prepared by Hexagon Transportation
Consultants, Inc. dated April 29, 2016 for the proposed mixed-use development at the 1409 El
Camino Real in Redwood City, California. This memorandum compares newly collected intersection
turning movement counts to the existing and future traffic volumes presented in the Redwood City
Downtown Precise Plan Draft Environmental Impact Report (DPP DEIR). The purpose of this
analysis is to establish whether the transportation impact conclusions from the DPP DEIR are still
valid and to determine if changes in traffic conditions within the downtown could result in new
significant impacts.

Project Information
The project site is located at the southeast corner of El Camino Real (SR 82) and Diller Street, and
is approximately 1.6 acres. The project area falls within the plan area of the Redwood City
Downtown Precise Plan (DPP). Currently, the project site is occupied by an auto repair shop, a
veterinary hospital, a hair salon, a treadmill outlet and an office building. The proposed project
would demolish the existing uses and construct an 8-story apartment building with 350 apartments
and 5,972 square feet of retail space.

Data Collection
On Tuesday, March 22, 2016, new manual turning movement counts were collected for four study
intersections during the AM and PM peak hours. Within Redwood City, traffic volumes typically
peak between 7:00-9:00 AM and between 4:00-6:00 PM. The four study intersections are listed
below.
      1. El Camino Real and Jefferson Avenue
      2. Middlefield Road and Jefferson Avenue
      3. Middlefield Road and Main Street
      4. Main Street and WB Woodside Road Ramp
The intersection turning movement counts are provided in Attachment 1.

Intersection Turning Movement Data Comparison
The new traffic counts were compared to the existing (year 2010) volumes and cumulative (year
2030) traffic volumes in the DPP DEIR. Table 1 and Table 2 present the results from the AM and
PM peak hour traffic analysis, respectively. The peak hour volume comparisons are discussed
below.
AM Volume Summary
As shown in Table 1, the 2016 AM peak hour traffic volumes vary from three percent lower to
twelve percent higher than the 2010 traffic volumes. The 2016 traffic volumes at the intersection of
El Camino Real/Jefferson Avenue are approximately 100 vehicles less than the 2010 traffic
volumes. The Middlefield Road/Jefferson Avenue intersection shows a decrease of approximately
50 vehicles between the year 2010 and year 2016 counts. The Middlefield Road/Main Street
intersection shows an increase of approximately 150 vehicles from the year 2010 to the year 2016.
At the Main Street/WB Woodside Road ramp intersection, traffic volumes increased by
approximately 90 vehicles from the year 2010 to the year 2016. The data show that some
intersections in the vicinity of the proposed project have experienced a small increase in AM peak-
hour traffic volumes since the DPP DEIR was prepared in 2010. The increase in traffic volumes is to
be expected as some developmental projects envisioned in the Redwood City Downtown Precise
Plan have been built. At all four intersections, the 2016 traffic volumes are well below the estimated
2030 cumulative traffic volume projections presented in the DPP DEIR. Based on this comparison,
there is no evidence that there is any change in circumstances that would require altering the level
of service conclusions presented in the DPP DEIR.
PM Volume Summary
As shown in Table 2, the 2016 PM peak hour traffic volumes vary from 17 percent lower to 8
percent higher than the 2010 traffic volumes. The 2016 traffic volumes at the intersection of El
Camino Real/Jefferson Avenue are approximately 800 vehicles less than the 2010 traffic volumes.
The Middlefield Road/Jefferson Avenue intersection shows a decrease of approximately 380
vehicles between the year 2010 and year 2016 counts. The Middlefield Road/Main Street shows a
decrease of approximately 200 vehicles from the year 2010 to the year 2016. At the Main
Street/WB Woodside Road ramp intersection, traffic volumes increased by approximately 90
vehicles from the year 2010 to the year 2016. The data show that PM peak-hour traffic volumes
have increased slightly at one intersection in the vicinity of the proposed project since the DPP
DEIR was prepared in 2010. As stated above, some development projects envisioned in the
Redwood City Downtown Precise Plan have been built between the year 2010 and the year 2016.
At all four intersections, the 2016 traffic volumes are well below the estimated 2030 cumulative
traffic volume projections presented in the DPP DEIR. Based on this comparison, there is no
evidence that there is any change in circumstances that would require altering the level of service
conclusions presented in the DPP DEIR.
